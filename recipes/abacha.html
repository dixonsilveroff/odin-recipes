Abacha (African Salad)

Description

African salad (Abacha food) is a delicious west African food that is native to the ndi Igbo (people of Igbo). I can prepare this delicacy within 15 minutes and it requires just cassava flakes, palm oil, crayfish, ugba and some other spices.
Abacha is very popular in the eastern part of Nigeria; it is one of the most Popular Igbo Foods; I donâ€™t visit Eastern part of the country and come back without having a taste of this Nigerian delicacy.

Ingredients

  • 6 to 8 cups of Abacha (African Salad)
  • Ehu 3 seeds (optional)
  • 2 cups Of Ugba (Ukpaka)
  • Half cup of crayfish
  • 1 seasoning cube
  • 150ml of palm oil
  • Edible powdered potash(1 teaspoon)
  • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • 3 garden eggs (optional)
  • Utazi leaves (optional)
  • Garden egg leaves (optional)
  • Meat/dry fish or stock fish

Procedures

  1. Blend your crayfish and also slice the onions, garden egg leaves.
  2. It is advisable to dissolve the powdered potash in about half a cup of clean water; this is a trick I employ just filter out unwanted solid materials that are often embedded in potash.
  3. Filter the dissolved potash into a mortar or pot, leaving out the residue.
  4. Add about 100ml of palm oil and stir to form a yellowish paste (ncha, as addressed by the igbos).
  5. Then add the ground crayfish and pepper, stir, then add the ground ehu if you like.
  6. Add the ugba to the mixture and stir, and then add the abacha, salt.
  7. Heat for a few seconds and serve
